CONSTITUTION OF THE SOVEREIGNTY

1. Fundamental Freedoms

All citizens inside of the Sovereignty hold fundamental rights and freedoms that are guaranteed by this Constitution and protected by the authority of the state.

(a) Freedom of conscience, including being able to think and believe, subject only to limitations necessary to protect public safety and constitutional order,
(b) Freedom of speech, including the right to communicate opinions and information across interstellar media, subject to regulation necessary to prevent persuasion into doing unwell actions, sabotage, or harm in any way,
(c) Freedom of peaceful assembly, subject to reasonable restrictions required to ensure public safety, and
(d) Freedom of association, including the creation of collective groups, however such associations should not seek to undermine constitutional order, cause harm, or public stability.

×

2. Mobility Rights

Freedom of movement is recognized as necessary to economic strength, social unification, and stability.

(a) Every citizen has the right to move freely and take up residence on any world or territory within the Sovereignty, though temporary restrictions may be imposed for security, environmental, or logistical reasons.
(b) The state shall own, operate, and maintain a nationalized interstellar transportation network spanning the Twenty Systems and potential further systems, designated as important infrastructure.
(c) Transit fares shall be regulated to ensure universal affordability. Mobility shall not be denied on the basis of economic status, except where necessary for security or emergency response.

×

3. Legal Rights, State Authority, and Personal Security

The security of the individual and the security of the Sovereignty are recognized as interdependent.

(a) Every person has the right to life, freedom, and security of the person, including authority over their own body and biology.
(b) No medical or biological modification shall be done without clear, informed, and voluntary consent, except where emergency intervention is required to preserve life.
(c) The state and its authorized sectors, including the military and security services, are allowed to conduct searches, surveillance, and investigations where reasonable suspicions exist to believe that a threat to public safety, law and order, or constitutional order may be present.
(d) Such actions must be done in agreement with law, balance in order, and operational necessity.
(e) Where state action results in damage, loss, or infringement upon an individual later determined to be innocent, the state shall provide full remediation, including: Formal acknowledgment of error, Restitution or replacement of damaged property where possible, Financial compensation where restoration is not possible.
(f) No person shall be detained without lawful justification, and any detained individual shall be informed without delay of the reasons for such action.

×

4. Equality Rights

(a) All persons are equally before and under the law and are entitled to equal protection and equal benefit of the law.
(b) Equality shall be held without discrimination based on origin, race, gender, age, disability, or physical condition, except where different treatment is strictly necessary for medical, logistical, or safety reasons.

×

5. Social and Economic Guarantees

The Sovereignty recognizes that stability in society and public confidence are bound to economic well-being.

(a) Universal healthcare shall be provided to all citizens and shall be free at the point of use.
(b) The state shall guarantee access to food, water, shelter where possible, and energy as essential resources necessary for life and civil order.
(c) Logistics and freight systems shall remain nationalized critical utilities, operated at low cost to ensure continuous and efficient distribution across the domestic Fellant market.
(d) The international deliveries and trade will be primarily operated through state-owned freight, with military craft being of escort to bulk transport.

×

6. The Orientation Mandate

Civil participation is recognized as a base of mutual trust between the state and the people.

(a) Every citizen, once reached eighteen years of age shall complete two months of mandatory state service, known as the Orientation.
(b) The Orientation shall teach infrastructure support, emergency response, piloting, logistics, and civil defense, reinforcing social unity and security.
(c) No more than twenty percent of a planet’s eighteen year old population may be undergoing the Orientation simultaneously, ensuring economic and social stability.
(d) Citizens hold the right to delay the Orientation for up to two years, for reasons of family, personal, or other causes.
(e) Citizens residing in colonies may complete Orientation on any Fellant world of their choice, with all travel costs covered by the state.

×

7. State Personnel and Authority

The operational authority of the Sovereignty is exercised through permanent professional sectors.

(a) The Active Force, comprising approximately one to two percent of the total population, shall be responsible for: Interstellar and planetary defense, Search and rescue operations, Protection and operation of strategic logistics and freight networks, Internal security operations when civil stability is at risk.
(b) The Civil Service, comprising approximately one-half to one percent of the population, shall be responsible for: Healthcare and emergency services (Such as but not limited to police, medical team, firefighting), Infrastructure management, Planetary and system-level administration.
(c) Both bodies shall operate under constitutional authority and are accountable to civil law and constitutional rules.

×

8. Cooperative Governance and Minimal Interference

(a) The Sovereignty must only govern through specific systems instead of intrusive control, only interacting where and when necessary to preserve stability, safety, or constitutional order.
(b) The state must respect personal performance, culture, and private property, except where lawful intervention is required due to suspicion of harm, criminal activity, or other risks.
(c) Governments should prioritize prevention, resilience, and cooperation rather than oppression, creating a better life through material security, civilian participation, and transparent authority.

×

9. Supremacy of the Constitution

(a) This Constitution is the supreme law of the First Federated Sovereignty of Fellant.
(b) Any law, regulation, or state action inconsistent with this Constitution shall be void and without force or effect.
(c) All legislative or structural changes shall be evaluated to ensure they do not undermine civil trust, essential services, or the operational strength of the Sovereignty.

×

10. State Emergency Authority (SEA)

(a) In the event of declared war, military conflict, or a verified threat to the Sovereignty, the state is authorized to undergo expanded powers necessary to ensure survival, defense, and continuity of the nation.

(b) During such periods, the state may: Create temporary restrictions on movement, trade, and assembly; Expand surveillance, intelligence gathering, and security operations; Claiming property, infrastructure, or labor necessary for defense and logistics; Direct and prioritize economic, industrial, and logistical output; Issue binding emergency directives with immediate effect.

(c) All emergency powers shall be exercised solely for the duration and purpose of the declared threat and shall be proportionate to the necessity of the situation.

(d) No emergency authority shall permit: Random, unreasoned execution or indefinite detention without cause; Permanent suspension of constitutional rights; Irreversible change of civil or biological status without consent, except where necessary to preserve life.

(e) Upon the end of hostilities or threat, all emergency measures shall automatically expire, and normal constitutional governance shall be fully restored.

(f) Actions taken under emergency authority will be subject to post-conflict review, and where state action is found to have caused unreasonable harm to innocent people, compensation shall be provided.
